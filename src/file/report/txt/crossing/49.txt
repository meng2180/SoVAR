This crash occurred on a two-lane undivided roadway that curved to the right with a super elevation of 6.3 percent. The roadway at this point had a downhill grade of 4.9 percent and there was an intersecting side road on the left. The posted speed limit was 89KMPH (55MPH). The roadway was dry and the weather was cloudy. This crash occurred on a weekday in the early hours of the morning before dawn. The roadway was not illuminated.Vehicle #1, a 2000 Peterbilt tractor-trailer, was traveling north on the two-lane roadway when it came up upon Vehicle #2, a 1998 Chevrolet pickup truck, which was facing north getting ready to make a left turn onto the side road. In an attempt to avoid a collision, the driver of Vehicle #1 steered to the left while locking up his brakes. As he did this, Vehicle #2 initiated its left turn. Vehicle #1 struck Vehicle #2 on the left side passenger compartment with its front right. Vehicle #1 skidded off the roadway to the left, impacting a road sign and came to rest off the roadway on the west side, north of the side road. Vehicle #2 continued off the roadway to the northwest and came to final rest in front of Vehicle #1.Vehicle #1 was driven by a belted 43-year old male who was not injured. This driver stated that Vehicle #2 was stopped in the roadway and he steered to the left to avoid striking it, as he had no room to maneuver to the right of Vehicle #2.
