This two-vehicle collision occurred mid-afternoon in a three-way intersection.  Southbound traffic was controlled by a stop sign traffic device.  Road surface dry asphalt, no adverse weather condition or sight restrictions. The posted speed limit 48 kMmph (30 mph). The east/west roadway has a +8% grade.Vehicle one (V1), a 1994 Yamaha 600cc motorcycle driven by a 36 year-old male, was traveling west. Vehicle two (V2), a 1993 Ford Crown Victoria sedan driven by a 39 year-old male with three passengers, was traveling south in the process of making a left turn after stopping at a stop sign, As V2 entered the intersection V1, according to witnesses was driving at a high rate of speed, changed lanes to the left and collided with the left side of V2. The driver of V1 came to final rest at the northwest corner of the intersection and the motorcycle came to rest in the intersection at point of impact. V2 came to rest facing east in the westbound lanes. Both  V1 and V2 were towed from the scene, the driver of V1 was transported to a local medical facility.The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other vehicle encroachment, from crossing street, turning into opposite direction. The critical reason for the critical event was not coded to this vehicle.  Associative factors for V 1 driver are as follows: driver performed various decision errors (aggressive driving behaviors).  V 1 driver violated a traffic signal, accelerated excessively  while weaving in and out of travel lanes, and failed to look far enough ahead for possible crossing traffic.  Medical reports indicated V1 driver  had an abnormal level of amphetamine.
