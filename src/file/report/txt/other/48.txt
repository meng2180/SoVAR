This two vehicle daytime collision occurred mid-morning at a four-leg intersection comprised by a north/south roadway and an east/west roadway.  The north/south roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of three lanes.  The east/west roadway is undivided, dry, level, and asphalt.  The east leg consists of five lanes.  The west leg consists of six lanes, including a right-turn only lane.  Both roadways are designated 56 kmph (35 mph) zones.  The intersection is controlled by on-color signals that were functioning properly during the collision.  Vehicle one (V1) is a 1994 Nissan Pathfinder sport utility vehicle driven by a twenty five (25) year old female.  V1 was traveling northbound in the number two lane.  Vehicle two (V2) is a 1984 Plymouth Reliant, two-door sedan.  V1 was driven by a sixty four (64) year old female with one passenger who was  traveling southbound in the number one lane.  As V1 attempted a left turn, the front plane of V2 contacted its right plane.  V1 then rolled unto its left plane.  V1 came to rest on its left plane facing northwest, blocking the eastbound number two and three lanes.  V2 came to rest facing southwest, blocking the eastbound number three and four lanes.  V1 received rollover damage and was towed from the scene.  V2 received moderate damage and was driven to the side of the roadway prior to being towed.The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection.  The critical reason for critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: external distraction.  The driver of V1 was looking left at pedestrians at the northwest corner of the intersection.  They did not see V2 until just before impact.  The driver was on her way from home to pick up her grandmother.
