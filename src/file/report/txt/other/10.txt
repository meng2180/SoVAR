This two-vehicle crash occurred on a three-lane, straight, level, undivided roadway at a driveway. There is one through lane in the westbound direction. The speed limit on this roadway is 64 kmph (40 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.Vehicle one (V1), a 2005 Chevrolet C2500 pickup towing a trailer, was westbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 2007 Toyota Camry, was stopped westbound in lane one, waiting to turn left into the driveway. There was a non-contact vehicle stopped behind V2. The driver of V1 applied the brakes and steered left in an attempt to avoid the non-contact vehicle. V1 traveled into the eastbound travel lane as V2 began to make the left turn. The right side of V1 struck the left side of V2. Both vehicles traveled off the southwest corner. The front right wheel/tire of V2 struck the curb. The front of V1 struck a newspaper delivery box. The right side of V2 struck a wooden sign. The right side of V1 struck a mailbox. The front of V2 struck a shrub. V1 was towed due to damage.The driver of V1, a forty-year-old male, had stopped at his workplace to pick up his trailer and was on his way to another town. He was well rested, healthy and familiar with his vehicle. He travels this route daily. This driver stated that he had previously told his boss that the brakes on the trailer did not work and assumed that they were fixed. When he realized that the trailer brakes were still not working, he steered left.
