This two vehicle daytime collision occurred just past noon on a north/south roadway.  The roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of two lanes.  The roadway is designated a 56 kmph (35 mph) zone.  Vehicle one (V1) is a 2004 Chevrolet Classic, four-door sedan, driven by a seventy one (71) year old female.  V1 was traveling northbound in the number one lane.  Vehicle two (V2) is a 2002 Mack Freedom, Cab-Over-Engine straight cargo truck that was legally parked along the east curb.  As V1 was traveling her cell phone began to ring and as the driver looked to retrieve it she drifted to the right causing the front plane to contacted the rear plane of V2's cargo area.  V1 came to rest facing northbound blocking the number one lane.  V2 came to rest facing northbound in the parking lane.  V1 received major damage and was towed from the scene.  V2 received moderate damage was driven from the scene.  The driver was coming from a hardware store and going to lunch with her passenger.  She had concerns with her family due to a family members illness. Factor was a fatigue due to just 4.5 hours of sleep in the past 24 hours. The driver takes the medication Lipitor for high cholesterol.  She rarely drives this route.
