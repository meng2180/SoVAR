This two vehicle daytime collision occurred shortly after midday at a four-leg intersection comprised by a north/south roadway and an east/west roadway.  The north/south roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of four lanes, including a right-turn only lane. The east/west roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of seven lanes.  Both roadways area designated 56 kmph (35 mph) zones.  The intersection is controlled by on-color signals that were functioning at the time of the collision.  Vehicle one (V1) is a 2000 Honda Odyssey mini-van, driven by a thirty six (36) year old male who was traveling southbound in the number two lane.  Vehicle two (V2) is a 1993 Ford Escort two-door hatchback, driven by a sixty five (65) year old female who was traveling westbound in the number three lane.  As V2 entered the intersection, the front plane of V1 contacted its right plane.  Both vehicles rotated clockwise as they traveled towards the southwest corner.  V1 came to rest facing northeast, blocking the eastbound number one lane.  V2 came to rest facing northeast, blocking the eastbound number two and three lanes.  V1 suffered moderate damage and was towed from the scene.  V2 received major damage and was towed from the scene.
