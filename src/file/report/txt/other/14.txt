This two-vehicle crash occurred on a two way, two-lane roadway in a residential area. There is one westbound and one eastbound lane that are not divided. The speed limit on the roadway is posted 40 kmph (25 mph).  The roadway is straight, level and constructed of bituminous asphalt. The crash occurred during early evening hours in a lighted area on a weekday. The roadway was dry and the sky was clear at the time of the crash.  Vehicle one (V1), a 1988 Nissan Pathfinder with one occupant, was traveling eastbound at approximately 40 kmph (25 mph). Vehicle two (V2), a 2004 Nissan Armada, was legally parked on the right side of the road.  V1 drifted to the right and struck the rear of V2 with its front. The impact forced V1, to rotate clockwise until it departed the right side of the roadway. V1 came to rest with the front tires on the sidewalk and the rear tires on the roadway facing in a southerly direction. The right front tire detached from the vehicle and came to rest near the right rear tire. V2 was pushed forward and came to rest facing in an easterly direction.  The vehicles came to rest within 3 meters (9.8 feet) of each other.  Both vehicles were towed from the scene. A twenty (20) year old male drove V1. He said that he was on his way home after leaving a convenience store about 1.6 kilometers (1 mile) away, to drop off cigarettes to his roommate.  He said that after he dropped off the cigarettes, he was going to work. The driver had to be at work at 2100 and works until 0700 as a security guard.  He was driving is his roommates vehicle and he drives it on a regular basis. He said the vehicle has no defects that he knows of.  He was traveling eastbound when a cat crossed the roadway from his left. He tried to avoid running over the cat by steering to the right and his vehicle then hit the parked vehicle on the side of the roadway.
