This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-legged intersection in an interchange area. The roadway south of the intersection consists of two lanes in each direction. The northbound approach lane curves to the left, has a downhill grade and is controlled by a stop sign, with a secondary sign on the post "Cross traffic does not stop". The east/west roadway is straight, level and undivided. The westbound approach consists of a left turn lane and one through lane. The speed limit on both of these roadways is 56 kmph (35 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.Vehicle one (V1), a 2007 Lexus IS350, was northbound in lane one, stopped at the stop sign. Vehicle two (V2), a 1995 Nissan Maxima, was westbound in lane one. Several non-contact vehicles were stopped westbound in lane two, waiting to make a left turn. As V1 proceeded through the intersection, the right side was struck by the front of V2. Both vehicles rotated clockwise. The back left wheel of V1 struck the curb on the northwest corner. Both vehicles were towed due to damage.The driver of V1, a twenty-three-year-old female, was traveling from her home to work. She was healthy, well rested and familiar with her vehicle. She travels this route daily. This driver stated that she was focused on the song on the radio prior to the crash. She stated that she did not see V2 and that the driver of the lead non-contact vehicle "waved her through," indicating that the intersection was clear. This driver was also eating oatmeal that was in a cup in her lap.
