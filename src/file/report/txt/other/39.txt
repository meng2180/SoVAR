This two vehicle collision occurred during the darkness of a pre-dawn on a four-leg intersection comprised by a north/south roadway and an east/west roadway.  The north/south roadway is undivided, dry, and level.  The north leg of the north/south roadway consists of two lanes and is comprised of asphalt.  The south leg of the north/south roadway is a concrete, one-lane, entrance to a school.  The east/west roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of seven lanes.  The north/south roadway is designated a 40 kmph (25 mph) zone.  The east/west roadway is designated a 56 kmph (35 mph) zone.  The intersection is controlled by on-color signals that were functioning properly at the time of the collision.  Vehicle one (V1) is a 2001 Chevrolet Tahoe sport utility vehicle, driven by a thirty (30) year old female with one passenger.  V1 was traveling eastbound in the number four (left-turn) lane.  Vehicle two (V2) is a 1999 Toyota Rav-4 driven by a forty six (46) year old female.  V2 was traveling westbound in the number one lane.  As V1 attempted the left turn, its right plane was contacted by the front plane of V2.  V1 rotated counter-clockwise as V2 rotated clockwise.  The right plane of V1 then contacted the left plane of V2.  V1 came to rest facing northwest at the northwest corner.  V2 came to rest facing northwest blocking the southbound number one lane. Both vehicles were towed from the scene.The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection.  The critical reason for th critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: misjudgment of gap or other's speed.
