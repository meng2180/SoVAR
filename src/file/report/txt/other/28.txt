This two-vehicle crash occurred on an undivided and level roadway. This roadway consists of two lanes in each direction with parking lanes on both sides. In the precrash area, the southbound lanes are curved to the left (the radius of curvature is 375 meters). The speed limit on this roadway is 40 kmph (25 mph). This crash occurred during daylight hours with no adverse atmospheric conditions.Vehicle one (V1), a 2000 Toyota Tundra, was southbound in lane one. Vehicle two (V2), a 1996 Nissan Pathfinder, was parked on the right (west) side of the southbound roadway. V1 traveled to the right and the front struck the back of V2. V1 was towed due to damage.The driver of V1, a twenty-six-year-old male, was not interviewed. He stated to the police officer that he was looking down at a picture just prior to the crash. When he looked back up and realized that he was heading toward V2, he applied the brakes, skidding longitudinally. No further information is available for this driver.
