This two vehicle collision occurred at a four-leg intersection comprised by a north/south roadway and an east/west roadway.  The north/south roadway is dry, level, asphalt and consists of two lanes.  The east/west roadway is dry, level, asphalt and consists of five lanes.  Railroad tracks are located west of the intersection.  A warning sign, located east of the intersection, informs westbound traffic of the approaching railroad tracks.  The north/south roadway is designated a 40 kmph (25 mph) zone.  The east/west roadway is designated a 56 kmph (35 mph) zone.  Vehicle one (V1) is a 2004 Nissan Altima sedan, driven by a thirty six (36) year old male with one passenger.  V1 was traveling westbound in the number two lane.  Vehicle two (V2) is a 2002 Ford Taurus sedan, driven by a forty eight (48) year old female was traveling westbound in the number two lane.  V1 wanted to change lanes to the right and while looking over her shoulder to the right checking for traffic but when she looked back V2 had stopped for a red light. The front plane of V1 contacted the rear plane of V2.  V1 came to rest facing northbound blocking the number one lane.  V2 came to rest facing westbound blocking the intersection.  V1 was towed from the scene due to damage.  V2 received moderate damage and was not towed. The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded:  other motor vehicle in lane, other vehicle stopped.  The critical reason was assigned to V1 as a driver related factor: external distraction.   An associated factor for V1 was inattention, for talking to the passenger.
