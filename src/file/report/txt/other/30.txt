This two vehicle mid-afternoon daylight collision occurred on a north/south roadway.  The roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of five lanes.  The roadway is designated a 56 kmph (35 mph) zone. Vehicle one (V1) is a 1998 Oldsmobile Intrigue, sedan driven by a seventeen (17) year old male with three passengers.  V1 was traveling northbound in the number two lane.  Vehicle two (V2) is a 2006 Chevrolet Silverado pickup driven by a forty four (44) year old male.  V2 was traveling northbound in the number two lane.  As V2 was traveling, it began to slow for traffic.  V1 swerved right unto the number one lane to avoid V2.  The right front tire of V1 contacted the east curb, but did not produce any damage.  V1 then re-entered the number one lane and traveled towards the number two lane.  The front plane of V1 then contacted the right plane of V2.  V1 then rotated counter-clockwise as it headed towards the east curb.  The left plane of V1 contacted the east curb.  V1 came to rest facing southbound blocking the number one lane.  V2 continued traveling northbound until pulling off the roadway.  V1 received major damage and was towed from the scene.  V2 received moderate damage and was observed driven from the scene.  The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded:  this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on the left side of travel lane.  The critical reason was assigned to V1 as a driver related factor: aggressive driving behavior.    Associated factors for V1 were: driver inexperience, speeding, overcompensation and poor directional control.   When he decided to pass V2 on the right, the driver was looking in his side mirror for approaching traffic.  He then made the lane change to the right and failed to maintain control of his vehicle.
