This two-vehicle crash occurred in a four-leg, traffic signal controlled intersection. Both roadways were straight and level. The east/west roadway has five lanes and each direction consists of two through lanes and a left turn lane. There is a westbound right turn lane at the intersection. The north/south roadway has five lanes; each direction consists of two through lanes and a left turn lane. The speed limit for the east/west roadway is posted 56 kmph (35 mph). The speed limit on the north/south roadway is posted 72 kmph (45 mph). This crash during the hours of darkness. The intersection is lighted with overhead street lights. There were no atmospheric conditions that contributed to this crash.  Vehicle one (V1), a 1997 Nissan Maxima 4 door with one occupant, was making a left turn from the northbound left turn lane.  Vehicle two (V2), a 1998 Plymouth Breeze 4 door with one occupant, was traveling southbound in the number one lane. V1 made a left turn after being stopped in the intersection across the path of V2. The front of V2 struck the right side of V1. The impact forced V1 to rotate counterclockwise coming to rest at the west side of the intersection in the eastbound number two lane facing east. V2 came to a controlled stop in a parking of a gas station at the southwest corner of the intersection. V1 was towed due to damage. V2 was driven from the scene. The driver of V1, a nineteen (19) year old female, was traveling from her place of employment to her home. She said that she had stopped in the intersection for about 3 seconds. She said she saw V2 approaching in the number one lane. She said that it looked like the other car was going to stop. She then made a left turn and was looking in the direction her car was turning and then was hit. She said that the light turned yellow just as she began to turn left. She said that she thought the other car was going to stop.
