This two vehicle collision occurred mid-morning.  The collision occurred at a four-leg intersection comprised by an east/west roadway, an interstate off-ramp, and an interstate on-ramp.  The east/west roadway is undivided, and dry. Traffic flow is controlled by on-colors signals. The west leg is concrete.  The east leg is asphalt.  The east leg has a 1.6% uphill slope as it travels towards the intersection.  The east leg consists of seven lanes.  The west leg consists of four lanes.  Both ramps are one-way, undivided, dry, asphalt, and level at the intersection.  The east/west roadway is designated a 56 kmph (35 mph) zone.  Vehicle one (V1) is a 1997 Lexus ES300, four-door sedan, driven by a twenty one (21) year old female who was  traveling eastbound in the number three lane.  Vehicle two (V2) is a 1971 Buick LeSabre, two-door convertible, driven by a forty seven (47) year old male with one passenger. V2 was traveling westbound in the number one lane.  As V1 attempted a left turn onto the interstate on-ramp, the front of V2 contacted its right plane.  V1 rotated counter-clockwise and came to rest facing northwest blocking the westbound number one lane.  V2 came to rest facing northwest blocking the interstate on-ramp.  V1 received moderate damage and was towed from the scene.  V2 received major damage and was towed from the scene.  The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded:  this vehicle traveling, turning left at Intersection.  The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: false assumption of other's actions.  The driver was looking at the traffic light and as it changed, he made the left turn thinking V2 would yield the right of way to his vehicle.  Associated factors for V1:failed to yield the right of way, also the driver was unlicensed,  The driver was on an errand going from home to a hospital and had concerns about his family and the new job he started the previous week.
