This two vehicle daytime collision occurred during early morning hours on the approach to a T-intersection comprised by a north/south roadway and an east/west roadway.  The north/south roadway is undivided, dry, level, and consists of two lanes.  The east/west roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of five lanes.  The north/south roadway is designated a 40 kmph (25 mph) zone.  The east/west roadway is designated a 56 kmph (35 mph) zone.  The only traffic control is a stop sign posted on the north/south leg of the intersection.Vehicle one (V1) is a 1994 Nissan Sentra, four-door sedan, driven by a thirty eight (38) year old unlicensed female that was traveling westbound in the number one lane.  Vehicle two (V2) is a 2007 Toyota Camry, four-door sedan, driven by a fifty four (54) year old male that was traveling westbound in the number two lane.  As V2 changed lanes to the right, the front plane of V1 contacted its right plane.  V1 then contacted the north curb of the intersection's east leg.  V1 continued across the intersection's north leg towards the west curb.  The front plane of V1 contacted the west curb and the foliage located along the curb.  V1's left plane contacted a fire hydrant as it continued to travel unto the sidewalk.  The front of V1 contacted a brick wall and a bush as it came to rest facing northbound on the sidewalk.  V1 was listed on the police report as driven with major damage; however, the vehicle was towed from the scene.  V2 received moderate damage and was driven from the scene.  The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded: other vehicle encroachment; from adjacent lane (same direction) - over left lane line. The critical reason not coded to this vehicle.  Associated factor for V1 inattention, drinking coffee and panicked due to encroachment by V2.
