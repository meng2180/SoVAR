This two vehicle daytime collision occurred midday on a north/south roadway.  The roadway is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and consists of five lanes.  The roadway is designated a 56 kmph (35 mph) zone.  Vehicle one (V1) is a 1998 Honda CR-V, sport utility vehicle, driven by a forty five (45) year old male.  V1 was entering the roadway from a private driveway.  Vehicle two (V2) is a 2000 Toyota Sienna, mini-van, driven by an unknown age male who was traveling southbound in the number two lane.  A delivery van was parked against the west curb just north of the driveway as V1 attempted a left turn onto the roadway.  V1's front plane contacted the right plane of V2.  V1 came to rest facing eastbound blocking the southbound number two lane.  V2 came to rest facing southbound in the number three lane.  The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded:  this vehicle traveling, turning left at intersection. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as an environment related factor: view obstructed by other vehicles.  The driver of V1 never saw V2 prior to his turn due to the legally parked van that obstructed his view.  He was exiting his work place on his way to lunch.
