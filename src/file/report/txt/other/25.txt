This two vehicle collision occurred on a clear midday. The collision occurred on a north/south roadway that is undivided, dry, level, asphalt, and comprised of two lanes.  The roadway is designated a 40 kmph (25 mph) zone. Vehicle one (V1) was a 1998 Nissan Frontier pickup driven by a fifty five (55) year old male.  V1 was illegally parked along the west curb blocking a driveway.  Vehicle two (V2), a 2001 Chevrolet S-10 pickup driven by a seventy four (74) year old male, was traveling southbound in the number one lane.  As V2 was traveling, V1 merged onto the roadway from its parked position.  The front plane of V2 contacted the left plane of V1.  V1 came to rest facing southwest blocking the number one lane.  V2 came to rest facing southbound blocking the number one lane.   V2 received minor damage and was not towed from the scene due to damage. V1 was towed from scene. The critical pre-crash event for V1 was coded:  this vehicle traveling, over the lane line on the left side of travel lane. The critical reason for the critical event was coded to V1 as a driver related factor: inadequate surveillance (failed to look, looked but did not see).  The driver of V1 was in a hurry to keep up with his delivery schedule.   Before merging onto the roadway the driver used is side mirror to check for oncoming traffic.  He never saw V2 prior to impact.  The driver is also a handyman.  He had an argument with a customer about a plumbing job and they had argued about progress and the deadline.  He has high blod pressure and takes the medication Norcasc.  He also had a mild cold.
