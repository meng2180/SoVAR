     The crash occurred on a level two lane undivided roadway with a posted speed limit of 55 MPH (89 KPH).  The road was curved left with a radius of curvature of 375 meters and a superelevation of 1.8% for Vehicle #2. The road was curved right with a radius of curvature of 1875 meters and no superelevation for Vehicle #1.  Both vehicles had black on yellow curve warning signs with a suggested speed of 50 MPH.  The weather was cloudy.     Vehicle #1, a 2000 Chevrolet Cavalier, was traveling west on the two-lane roadway negotiating a curve to the right approaching a driveway.  Vehicle #2, a 1995 Dodge Intrepid, was traveling east on the same roadway negotiating the curve to the left.  Vehicle #1 attempted a left hand turn into the driveway and was struck in the front by Vehicle #2's front.  Vehicle #1 was rotated counterclockwise back across both lanes and came to rest in the westbound lane facing east.  Vehicle #2 continued off the right side of the roadway, came back on the roadway crossed both lanes, off the north side of the road and struck a 25cm pole with its front.  Vehicle #2 came to rest facing in a northerly direction.  Both vehicles were towed due to damage. Police did not consider either alcohol or illegal drugs to be involved and ordered no tests.     The Chevrolet Cavalier (Vehicle #1) was driven by a belted 80 year old male who was transported, treated and released for minor injuries.  The right front seat passenger was a belted 72-year-old female who was also transported, treated and released for minor injuries. Both driver and passenger airbags deployed. The interview was refused and no information obtained from the driver.
