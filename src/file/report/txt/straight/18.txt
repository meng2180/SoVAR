The crash occurred in the westbound lane of a two-way roadway with one lane in each direction.  The roadway was straight and level and had a speed limit of 48 kmph (30 mph).  It was the mid-evening hours and it was dark out but the roadway was lighted. It was dry with no adverse weather conditions.Vehicle #2 was a 1995 Ford Taurus that was traveling eastbound and veered into the westbound lane.  Vehicle #1 was a 2001 Ford F-150 pick-up truck traveling westbound on the same roadway.  The front of Vehicle #2 contacted the front of Vehicle #1.A 39-year old male drove the Ford F-150 (Vehicle #1) and he did not sustain any injuries.  The passenger seated in the front-right position sustained minor injuries and was transported to a medical facility.  The driver stated that the driver of Vehicle #2 had an alcohol odor about him and that he was driving eastbound in the westbound lane.  Vehicle #1 was towed due to damage.  The driver of this vehicle declined to participate in the study.
